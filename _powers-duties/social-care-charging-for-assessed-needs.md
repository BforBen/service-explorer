Local authorities may charge (under Section 14 of the Care Act) for meeting needs under sections 18 to 20 of the Act, but must provide community equipment (aids and minor adaptations) and, for the first 6 weeks only, intermediate care and reablement support services; services for adults suffering from variant Creutzfield-Jakob disease free of charge. LAs may charge for putting in place arrangements to meet needs, but no more than the costs incurred.
